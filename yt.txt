When you upload a video created with Google NotebookLM, such as an AI-generated "Deep Dive" audio conversation, you should keep YouTube's policies regarding synthetic and original content in mind.

### 1. Disclose Altered or Synthetic Content
YouTube requires creators to disclose content that is meaningfully altered or synthetically generated when it seems realistic. Since NotebookLM creates realistic-sounding audio, you must label the video during the upload process.

**How to disclose:**
1. Go to **YouTube Studio**.
2. Click **CREATE** and then **Upload videos**.
3. In the **Details** section, scroll down to **Altered content**.
4. Select **Yes** for the question: "Do any of the following describe your content? (Makes a real person appear to say or do something they didn't do, alters footage of a real event or place, or generates a realistic-looking scene that didn't actually occur)."
5. This will add a label to your video’s description letting viewers know it was synthetically generated.

### 2. Copyright and Source Material
While the audio is generated by NotebookLM, the "legal" aspect also depends on the sources you provided to the tool.
*   **Permissions:** Ensure you have the rights or permission to use the text, documents, or research you uploaded to NotebookLM to generate the video.
*   **Fair Use:** If you are using copyrighted materials for research, consider whether your final video transforms that content significantly.

### 3. Monetization Considerations
If you are part of the YouTube Partner Program (YPP) or plan to apply, your content must follow the **Reused Content** and **Inauthentic Content** policies:
*   **Originality:** YouTube values content that adds a unique perspective or commentary. Purely AI-generated videos with no original input or modification can sometimes be flagged as "repetitive" or "reused."
*   **Transformation:** To improve your chances for monetization, try to add your own original edits, visuals, or context to the NotebookLM output rather than uploading the raw AI file.

For more detailed information, you can visit these Help Center articles:
